Space

Space is a vacuum, meaning it contains almost nothing, but it is not completely empty. Space does not begin at a specific altitude above the Earth, but the Kármán line at 100 km is a commonly used definition.

The Origin

Word Origin and History for space. 1703, "to arrange at set intervals," from space (n.). Meaning "to be in a state of drug-induced euphoria" is recorded from 1968.
